Delhi

Where Empires Fell and Street Food Rose

About Delhi

Delhi, the capital of India, is a city layered with centuries of history, power, and resilience. From ancient kingdoms to Mughal empires and British colonial rule, Delhi has been rebuilt many times, each era leaving behind monuments, stories, and scars.

Today, Delhi stands as a dynamic blend of old and new. Grand historical structures exist beside glass towers, bustling markets, and metro lines. It is chaotic, intense, and unapologetically alive.

Monuments, Streets & Markets

Street Food Capital

Delhis food scene is legendary. From buttery parathas in Chandni Chowk to spicy chaat, kebabs, momos, and rich Mughlai dishes, the city feeds every craving imaginable.

Places like Paranthe Wali Gali, Jama Masjid, and local night markets serve food that is bold, messy, and unforgettable. Eating in Delhi is not a choice; it is a lifestyle.

Flavours of Delhi

Culture & Everyday Life

Delhi is multilingual, multicultural, and constantly moving. Art galleries, book cafs, political debates, street protests, and late-night food runs all coexist in the same city.

It is not gentle. It is not slow. But it is honest. Delhi challenges you, toughens you, and rewards those who stay curious.
